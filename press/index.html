Description

Great Cascade is an open world where characters remember and react to the things you do. Your actions matter.

Maybe you deliver an item for a guy. He gives you a reward and likes you a little more. Or maybe instead of delivering the item, you sell it to the highest bidder. Now the guy hates you. He tells his friends and they hate you. You're rich, but on the run.

It's not a morality system. It's not a scripted story with branching paths. It's a system of cause and effect. It's characters with unique personality traits, memories, and goals. Your actions build your story.

Give, take, save, kill, inform, hide, be loyal, betray. Do what you want, and deal with the consequences.

History

Great Cascade began as a bunch of ideas on dynamic character AI. It was fueled by talks from Clint Hocking on emergent gameplay and frustrations with Red Dead Redemption forcing me to kill folks for no good reason, or failing missions for killing the "wrong" person (love the game).

Real work began mid-2014. Character AI was the focus for the first year. Once I had a solid foundational system (oh, hey this works), I began on the visuals. I opted for an art style that I could execute relatively quickly and was somewhat in my wheelhouse.

Great Cascade is my first big game. Sinking so much time into a single project can be scary, but I'm really proud of how it shaping up.

Features

  • Open world, open story, single-player.
  • Characters remember and react to the actions you and other character take.
  • Every new game is different because the characters are different, so how you play and how the characters react will be unique.
  • Super pretty.
